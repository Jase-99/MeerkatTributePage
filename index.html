Meerkat Information

The meerkat (Suricata suricatta) or suricate is a small mongoose found in southern Africa.

Characterised by a broad head, large eyes, a pointed snout, long legs, a thin tapering tail, and a brindled coat pattern.

Meerkats are eusocial, and form packs of two to 30 individuals each that occupy home ranges around 5 km2

Members of a pack take turns at jobs such as looking after pups and keeping a lookout for predators.

Packs live in rock crevices in stony areas and in large burrow systems in plains.

Encounters between members of different packs are highly aggressive, leading to severe injuries and sometimes deaths.

Meerkat burrows are typically 5 m (16 ft) in diameter with around 15 openings.

Meerkats are generally tame animals. However, they are unsuitable as a pet as they can be aggressive and have a strong, ferret-like odour.

Meerkats spend nearly five to eight hours foraging every day. Like other social mongooses, meerkats in a pack will disperse within 5m.

The meerkat has a specialised thermoregulation system that helps it survive in its harsh desert habitat.

Meerkats are widely depicted in television, movies and other media.

They use a broad variety of calls to communicate among one another for different purposes, for example to raise alarm on sighting a predator.
